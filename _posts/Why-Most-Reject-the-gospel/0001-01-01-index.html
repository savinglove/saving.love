Why Most People Reject the gospel

This free gift's usually rejected.

The Bible says come just as you are.
Heaven's a free gift costing no money.
Everyone's undeserving.

The gospel:
Jesus died (a sacrifice paying sin penalty), got buried, and was risen.

Trusting Jesus died as a sacrifice for you (paying sin penalty), got
buried, and was risen will give you Heaven.

Trusting more for Heaven's a false gospel.

Everyone sins daily.
No human obeys God's standard or
stops sinning until Heaven.
The Bible has hundreds of laws.
No human can obey God's demands perfectly.

A few ideas why the gospel's shared, but dismissed:

These are just some ideas the gospel's dismissed:
I've done these all. Not everything's listed.

  • False gospels:
    Sometimes called a "free gift," false gospels often mention the death, burial, and rising of Jesus but require more to be saved.
  • Bad Explanation:
    Is necessary information left unshared? The simple gospel can be shared with simple words.
  • Unnecessary Information:
    Sharing the gospel can be fast. It's simple. Do they believe it's true, trusting it's enough?
  • Rude:
    Gentle, quiet words can help.
  • Behaving as if You Think You're Wise:
    Appearing as if you think you're wise is unreliable. Is it more successful to act like a friend?
  • Behaving like You Know Everything:
    Acting like you know everything is annoying.
  • Deceit:
    Tricking someone blocks trust.
  • Intimidating:
    Intimidation's uncomfortable, preventing agreement.
  • Silencing Questions:
    Some curiosity may need to be gone.
  • Religious Words:
    Weird words can prevent clear messages. Is "grace" understood? Can you use "look/pay attention" instead of "behold?"
  • Spam:
    Too much of something can be disregarded.
  • Using Church:
    Some churches share the gospel weekly... some churches expect everyone to somehow trust the good news already... even when most reject this free gift costing no money (Heaven doesn't cost money).

Most don't accept the free gift of Heaven.

  • Because: the gate's small, the way is narrow that leads to life, and only a few find it. (Matthew 7:14)

God chose who'll be saved.
God still made this free gift available to anyone who accepts the gospel.
God wants everyone saved.

  • Because we're united with Christ, we've received an inheritance from God, he chose us beforehand (predestined) and makes everything work out according to his will: (Ephesians 1:11)
  • No man can come to me unless the Father who sent me gathers them, and I'll raise them up on the last day. (John 6:44)
  • This is how much God loved the world: He gave his one and only Son spawned from human birth, that whoever believes in him won't perish but live forever. (John 3:16)
  • For the Son of Man came to seek and save the lost. (Luke 19:10)

Can repeated attempts at sharing the gospel make things worse?

Those who've already rejected the gospel may get annoyed at attempts repeated too quickly.
Share it with everyone at least once. The entire world.
Why spend your energy on one person?
You can share the gospel with the world.
